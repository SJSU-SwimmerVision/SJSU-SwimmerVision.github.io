We present the SJSU D1 Swim Dataset, an open source swim dataset. We use this dataset in addition to externally aquired data to build a swim stroke classification system and investigate pose estimation enhancements, with the ultimate goal of developing an automated technique feedback pipeline.

Abstract

Swim(mer) detection and pose estimation is a challenging problem in machine learning and artificial intelligence as the body of the swimmer is continuously submerged under the water. The objective of this paper is to enhance existing ML models for estimating swim poses and for recognizing strokes to aid swimmers in pursuing a more perfect technique. We introduce a new extensive swimming dataset and test a methodology augmenting raw video data and adjusting a YOLOv7 base model to enhance swim pose estimation. We found the standard multi-class classification using a single CNN/MLP to be insufficient for stroke recognition due to the similarity between strokes, so we designed a hierarchical binary classification tree using multiple ensembles of multilayer perceptron (MLP), Long short-term memory (LSTMs), CNN, and residual network (ResNet) models. Through these optimizations, the confidence level of pose estimation has increased by over 30\%, and the ensembles of our recognition model have achieved approximately 90\% accuracy. Fine-tuning of our recognition models and research combining joint keypoint coordinates with angle measurements as inputs could further increase the accuracy of our models.
